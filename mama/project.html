  • Here’s how to make Syrian Shawarma:

  • Ingrediants

  1. 500 grams chicken breast (cut into thin slices)
  2. 3 tablespoons yogurt
  3. 2 tablespoons vinegar
  4. tablespoons lemon juice
  5. tablespoon olive oil
  6. garlic cloves (minced)
  7. teaspoon ground cumin
  8. teaspoon paprika
  9. 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  10. 1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom
  11. Salt and pepper to taste

  • For serving:

  1. Pita bread or flatbread
  2. Garlic sauce (toum) or tahini sauce
  3. Sliced tomatoes, cucumbers, and pickles
  4. French fries (optional)

  • Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, mix the yogurt, vinegar, lemon juice, olive oil,
    2. garlic, cumin, paprika, cinnamon, cardamom, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken slices and ensure they are fully coated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours (or overnight for best flavor).
  2. Cook the Chicken: Heat a large pan or grill over medium heat. Add the marinated
    3. chicken slices and cook for about 8-10 minutes until they are browned and fully cooked
  3. Assemble the Shawarma: Place some cooked chicken on a piece of pita bread. Add
    4. garlic sauce or tahini sauce, then top with sliced tomatoes, cucumbers, pickles, and French fries if desired.
  4. Roll and Serve: Roll the pita bread tightly around the filling and serve the
    5. shawarma immediately.

  • Koshary

  1. cup rice (short or medium grain)
  2. cup brown lentils
  3. cup elbow macaroni or small pasta
  4. cup chickpeas (cooked or canned)
  5. large onions (thinly sliced)
  6. 3-4 garlic cloves (minced)
  7. 1 can tomato sauce (or homemade sauce)
  8. teaspoon coriander (ground)
  9. Salt and pepper (to taste)
  10. Oil (for frying and cooking)

  • Steps:

  1. Cook the lentils:
  2. Rinse the lentils under cold water. Boil them in a pot with salted water for
    3. about 15-20 minutes until they are soft but not mushy. Drain and set aside.

  • Cook the rice:

  1. Rinse the rice and cook it with 1½ cups of water, a pinch of salt, and a bit
    2. of oil. Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes. Set aside.

  • Cook the pasta:

  1. Boil the macaroni in salted water according to package instructions. Drain
    2. and set aside.

  • Fry the onions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and fry the sliced onions until golden and crispy. Set aside
    2. on a paper towel to drain excess oil. Reserve the oil for later.

  • Make the tomato sauce:

  1. In a saucepan, heat a little oil (you can use the oil from frying the onions),
    2. add the minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant. Add the tomato sauce, vinegar, cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 10-15 minutes. Adjust seasoning if needed.

  • Assemble the Koshari:

  1. In a large dish, layer the rice, lentils, pasta, and chickpeas. Top with crispy
    2. fried onions.
Serve the tomato sauce and extra fried onions on the side.

  • Serving:

  1. Koshari is usually served with a side of tomato sauce and optionally hot
    2. chili sauce.
